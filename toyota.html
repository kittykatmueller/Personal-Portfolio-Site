01 Context

Setting the scene

My Role

When I joined the European Toyota App team, Toyota was investing heavily in EV technology and launching a new energy management area within the app, designed to help users understand, control, and optimise energy between their car and home.

As Lead Product Designer, I owned the end-to-end experience, from discovery and research through to flow design, stakeholder alignment, and testing.

Team Setup

The work was happening within an established team structure and delivery culture that really pushed for "just implement it." My role became not just about redesigning the experience, but also introducing stronger design thinking and more realistic delivery talks, often directly with senior stakeholders at Toyota.

Business Objectives
  • Optimise vehicle charging costs with a seamless EV charging experience
  • Educate users about the benefits and capabilities of V1G technology
  • Develop user-friendly interfaces for smart charging controls that accommodate varying energy needs and preferences
02 User Research

Understanding the user

We conducted interviews with EV owners across Europe to understand their current charging habits, pain points, and mental models around energy costs. Key themes emerged around confusion with tariff structures, anxiety about charge levels, and lack of trust in automated systems.

03 Challenges

What we were up against

Smart charging sits at the intersection of complex energy tariffs, vehicle hardware constraints, and highly varied user needs. Communicating the value of ceding control to an automated system — while keeping users informed and in control — proved to be the core design challenge.

04 Looking to Competitors

Learning from the market

We audited smart charging flows from Volkswagen, Tesla, Octopus Energy, and Ohme. While most apps offered scheduling, few handled the onboarding of smart charging in a way that built genuine user confidence. This gap represented a clear opportunity to differentiate.

05 Solutions

What we built

We designed a four-step onboarding flow that walked users through setting a departure time, minimum charge level, and maximum charge — framing smart charging as a tool that works for them, not against them. Clear progress indicators and plain-language explanations reduced drop-off significantly in testing.

06 Outcomes

The impact

The redesigned smart charging flow launched to Toyota Connected users across Europe. Early data showed a marked increase in smart charging activation rates and a reduction in support contacts related to charging setup. Stakeholder feedback highlighted the onboarding as a benchmark for future energy features.
